Human Skills: Not Just Fluff

What surprised me most

What surprised me the most about Human Skills learning is that it was part of the course! I didn't expect a tech course to be in touch with mindfulness, communication and understanding learning strengths. I think it can be helpful in the tech field because of the change in employment requirements in the last decade. I studied veterinary nursing for 2 years and a key part of the course was trying to create graduates that are good at their job but also good at being a point of connection between an owner's animal and their pets' health. This can also be transferred to the tech industry.

Three ego checks
  • I get distracted by extending my knowledge in less useful areas
  • Sometimes I "just figure it out alone" and that slows me down
  • I care way too much about my website styling
Why empathy matters

It's the difference between being a coding robot and someone people actually want to work with. Empathy helps you build things real users need, not just what's cool to code.

The awkward bits

Admitting when I'm lost instead of pretending I get it. Also the reflection exercises feel cringe at first.

Why we bother with this

Because anyone can learn to code but devs who can communicate and collaborate get hired. Especially with AI booming, tech's changing too fast to rely on just technical skills.

Waste of time?

I used to think it was. Sometimes still feel like some exercises can be. But sacrificing 10% of coding time to get better at communication is a good trade off.
