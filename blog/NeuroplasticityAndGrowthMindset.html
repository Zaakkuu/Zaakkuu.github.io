Neuroplasticity Mindset

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Knowing that the brain can change and adapt helps people realize they aren’t stuck being "bad" at something. It turns learning into a process of building skills rather than hitting a wall.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

Going back to the javascript-kata until I can say I'm confident with all of the concepts and able to do them quicker.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Repeating learning tasks to indent information into your brain, new experiences (coding in a different text editor) or trying to get the same output with different lines of code.

Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.

https://health.clevelandclinic.org/neuroplasticity

How it helps:

Realizing my brain can change means I'm not just "bad" at coding and I'm still building those pathways.

How I'll use it:

  • Grinding out JavaScript katas until they feel natural
  • Trying the same problem different ways to force new connections

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is the belief that refining your skills comes through effort and practice, not predermined natural talent.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I didn’t know about growth mindsets before but I think I have one. Getting good at coding isn’t magic.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

Reframing struggles from “”i cant do this” to “i cant do this yet”
Viewing mistakes as part of learning, not proof I’m bad at coding,
Seeing challenges as chances to grow, not walls to hit,
And setting small goals
