Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health.

The 4 dimensions are:

  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whānau (family wellbeing)

With 4 walls, the wharenui is a symbol of these 4 dimensions. The wharenui has connections to the whenua (land, sea, natural world) which forms the foundations for the four dimensions

Wall Activity
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Going to the gym, and regular walking
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Call my parents when they our time zones overlap
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Practising my regular, controlled breathing exercises
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Express gratitude to those around me more often
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Visiting new areas of this beautiful country when I have the time
